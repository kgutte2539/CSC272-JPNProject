Hiragana

To understand the basics of Japanese, it is important to understand the alphabets first which is completely different from English or any other latin language alphabets. Japanese has three basic alphabet structure which are Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. To talk about these basic structures, we are not going to talk about the Kanji part just because Kanji comes from the stem of Hiragana and Katakana and also can be challenging until understanding these two basic alphabets first. To easily break down Hiragana and Katakana, simply think of lower case letters and upper case letters that we use with enlish because that's basically what Hiragana and Katakana are. Hiragana is more like lower case letter and Katakana is more upper case letters but most foreigners names and inventions are mainly used with Katakana which may feel weird but these are just the simple rules of Japanese so far.
