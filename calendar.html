Calendar in Japanese

Before we learn how to read and write Calendar, we must know the basics of the Days of the week. Learning days of the week isn't that difficult but there are kanji as well that's included and most people uses Kanji for Days of the Week.

Days of the Week in Japanese

Kanji Hiragana Romaji English
月曜日 げつようび Getsuyoubi Monday
火曜日 かようび Kayoubi Tuesday
水曜日 すいようび Suiyoubi Wednesday
木曜日 もくようび Mokuyoubi Thursday
金曜日 きんようび Kinyoubi Friday
土曜日 どようび Doyoubi Saturday
日曜日 にちようび Nichiyoubi Sunday

From looking at the Day of the Week table, you probably have guessed that the first letter of the kanji is only difference and the rest is the Youbi. As long you know the first kanji, it will make it easier to understand as long you memorize the first part.

When it comes to Days of the Month in Japanese it can be pretty tricky because it is similar to the normal counting in Japanese but also different in some cases. Most part that you have to memorize is 1-10, which has the most differences out of all of them. From 11-20, all you have to know that it different is the 14 and the 20. For the 21-30, the only part different from the normal counting is the 24, and other than that it will end in Nichi which means day.
